\section*{Supplementary Note 1. Evidence hierarchy}

The main manuscript interprets results through a hierarchy rather than treating every diagnostic row as an independent hypothesis test. Firearm suicide is the primary outcome. Total suicide, non-firearm suicide, firearm homicide, and total firearm deaths are secondary outcome-family checks. Firearm-law covariates, contextual covariate layers, staggered-adoption diagnostics, Arkansas recoding, leave-one-out influence, and placebo timing are sensitivity checks. Rurality, gun ownership, baseline-risk heterogeneity, and policy-feature interactions are exploratory. Cohort ATT rows, event-time rows, full legal source tables, and full robustness grids are appendix diagnostics.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.20\linewidth}p{0.31\linewidth}p{0.34\linewidth}}
\toprule
Tier & Items & Interpretation \\
\midrule
Primary outcome & Firearm suicide & Central mortality claim. \\
Secondary outcomes & Total suicide; non-firearm suicide; firearm homicide; total firearm deaths & Specificity and outcome-family interpretation. \\
Sensitivity checks & Firearm-law covariates; health-access and overdose covariates; Phase 3B2 covariate layers; staggered-adoption diagnostics; Arkansas recoding; leave-one-out; placebo timing & Tests stability and boundaries. \\
Exploratory checks & Rurality, gun-ownership, baseline-risk, and policy-feature heterogeneity & Hypothesis-generating only. \\
Appendix diagnostics & Cohort ATT rows; event-time rows; legal source table; full robustness grids & Transparency and audit trail. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section*{Supplementary Note 2. Legal audit and policy-feature coding}

The legal audit records one row per state and distinguishes clean source-verified within-panel adopters from verified non-adopters, partial cases, ambiguous cases, and baseline-permitless states. Partial, ambiguous, and baseline-permitless rows are documented so that they are not silently absorbed into the binary treatment definition.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lr}
\toprule

\midrule

Ambiguous reviewed & 1 \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Among the 26 clean source-verified adopter rows, 21 had a training requirement removed, 25 removed the carry-permit background-check screen, and 12 removed a permit-specific misdemeanor-violence screen. These policy-feature fields describe the carry-permit process and are treated as exploratory.

\section*{Supplementary Note 5. Firearm-law and contextual covariate sensitivity}

External firearm-law covariates attenuated but did not erase the firearm-suicide estimate. Demographic-poverty covariates also preserved the firearm-suicide and total-suicide estimates. The short HRSA mental-health-provider window did not retain statistical significance and should be interpreted as a short-window sensitivity rather than a full-panel replacement. These rows are covariate-layer sensitivity checks, not uniformly superior causal models, because some time-varying covariates may lie downstream of broader state policy environments.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrrr}
\toprule

\midrule

\section*{Supplementary Note 9. Heterogeneity and policy-feature checks}

Rurality was the clearest recurring heterogeneity dimension: firearm suicide, total suicide, and total firearm deaths had positive high-rurality interactions. These models are exploratory because state-level subgroup indicators are coarse and adoption is politically patterned.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
Outcome/dimension & Interaction & $p$ \\
\midrule
Firearm suicide, high rurality & 0.870 & 0.003 \\
Total suicide, high rurality & 1.325 & 0.002 \\
Total firearm deaths, high rurality & 1.630 & 0.026 \\
Non-firearm suicide, high gun ownership & 0.549 & 0.001 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Policy-feature heterogeneity did not identify a stable mechanism. None of the 15 training, carry-permit background-check, or misdemeanor-screen interaction rows had $p<0.05$. Five rows were sparse or unavailable because the feature-specific clean-adopter comparison group was absent.
